# Maringues (Sheet 670)

**Carte Geologique de la France, 1:50,000**
Authors: P. Dadet, M. Jeambrun (Paleozoic basement), L. Clozier, R. Fleury (sedimentary formations)
Coordination: D. Giot, BRGM (1974)

## Overview

Sheet 670 (Maringues) covers the western border of the **Montagne Bourbonnaise**, a schisto-granitic massif in the northern Massif Central, and the adjacent **Limagne basin** — an Oligocene graben. The area sits at the junction of two contrasting geological domains: a crystalline basement plateau to the east and a sedimentary basin to the west.

## Geographic Setting

- **Eastern half**: Granitic and metamorphic plateau at 400-850 m, pastoral/forested, low population
- **Western boundary**: Abrupt north-south fault scarp dropping into the Limagne
- **Limagne basin**: Three sub-regions separated by the Dore and Allier river valleys
  - Right bank: Eocene-Oligocene detrital formations (Gore), deeply eroded
  - Left bank: Gently undulating marl plain at 300-390 m ("Terres Noires" — dark soils)
  - Allier floodplain: 1.5-2 km wide, terraced from 258 m (south) to 285 m (north)

## Geological Units

### Paleozoic Basement

**Crystallophyllian Basement (oldest)**
- Schists and gneisses without fossils, associated with andesitic volcanic complex
- Gneiss, quartzite, mica schist associations

**Andesitic Complex of Arrones (Upper Devonian)**
- Andesites, lavas, tuffs, and cornean rocks
- Metamorphosed volcanic complex associated with schists and limestones
- Pre-Visean age, estimated by analogy with Morvan and Lyonnais series

**Volcano-sedimentary Formations (Visean)**
- Rhyolitic tuffs at the top (Visean superior, ~325 Ma)
- Fossiliferous tuffs with plant fossils and brachiopods (*Productus giganteus*)
- Breccia with spherulites (Visean middle)
- Fossiliferous greywackes and calcareous greywackes at base

**Siltites and Sandstones of the Ardoisiere Basin (Lower Visean)**
- Terrigenous sediments: alternating siltites and sandstones with conglomerate layers
- Deposited in a marine basin with ripple marks
- Contact metamorphism near granite creates spotted schists (cordierite)

### Igneous Rocks — Two Granitic Episodes

**1. Caledonian Granites (~506 Ma)**
- **Chateau-Gaillard granite**: Porphyritic monzonitic biotite granite
- Southern portion of the sheet, extends into neighboring Thiers sheet
- Zircon age: 506 +/- 18 Ma (Cantagrel et al., 1970)

**2. Hercynian Granites (~337 Ma)**
- **Bois Noirs granite (Lachaux type)**: Coarse-grained, alkaline, rich in quartz, poor in biotite
- Covers half the sheet's basement area, extends east toward the Forez
- Cataclastic texture, sometimes porphyroid
- Biotite age: 324-348 Ma (multiple K-Ar dates)
- Includes accessory minerals: zircon, monazite, sphene, uraninite, thorite

- **Busset/Mayet-de-Montagne granite**: Porphyritic, calc-alkaline with biotite and amphibole
- Dark, homogeneous, with large K-feldspar porphyroblasts (up to 10 cm)
- Contains amphibole (hornblende) — distinguishes it from Busset type
- Namurian age: ~310-321 Ma
- Intrudes and metamorphoses the volcano-sedimentary formations

### Tertiary Sediments

**Oligocene (Limagne basin fill)**
- Detrital formations from the Eocene-Oligocene collapse of the Limagne graben
- Mapped by lithological criteria and aerial photo preparation

**Sables et Argiles du Bourbonnais (Plio-Quaternary)**
- Sands and clays covering the plateau, derived from weathered basement

**Colluvial and Superficial Formations**
- Slope deposits, solifluxion materials

**Alluvial Formations**
- Allier and Dore river terraces and floodplain deposits
- Terraces step up to 120 m above present river level (oldest)

### Anthropogenic Formations
- Former iron slag deposits and mining waste

## Tectonics

- Major **N-S fault system** (Limagne fault) forming the western basin boundary
- NW-SE and NE-SW fracture systems compartmentalize the Oligocene sediments
- Faulted contacts between granite, andesitic complex, and sediments
- Sichon fault separates the Bois Noirs granite from the andesitic complex

## Resources

**Hydrogeology**
- Alluvial aquifer of the Allier (main water resource)
- Granitic fracture aquifers (limited)

**Mineral Resources**
- Uranium potential in Bois Noirs granite area
- Historical iron ore exploitation
- Quarry aggregates from granite and volcanic rocks

## Geochemistry

Includes detailed chemical analyses (major + trace elements) of:
- Caledonian granites (Chateau-Gaillard): SiO2 64-68%, calc-alkaline
- Hercynian granites (Bois Noirs/Lachaux): SiO2 72-76%, alkaline
- Hercynian porphyroid granites (Busset/Mayet): SiO2 67-69%, calc-alkaline
