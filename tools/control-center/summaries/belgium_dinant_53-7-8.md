# Hastiere - Dinant (Sheet 53/7-8)

**Carte Geologique de Wallonie, 1:25,000**
Authors: B. Delcambre & J.L. Pingot (1993)
Source: Ministere de la Region Wallonne

## Overview

Sheet 53/7-8 covers the southern part of the **Dinant Synclinorium**, a segment of the Condroz Nappe (allochthonous unit thrust over the Cambro-Silurian Brabant Massif). The Meuse River and its tributaries cut deeply into the gently undulating Condroz plateau, exposing Upper Paleozoic formations on their slopes — primarily terrigenous rocks from the Famennian and carbonate rocks from the Lower Carboniferous.

This is a historically significant area: numerous local and international stratotypes have been defined here. The term **"Dinantian"** is used worldwide to designate the Lower Carboniferous.

## Geological Setting

- **Position**: Between the Condroz and the Entre-Sambre-et-Meuse region
- **Elevation**: Undulating plateau at 180-250 m
- **Rivers**: Meuse (with large meanders), Hermeton, Lesse (more sinuous)
- **Structure**: East-west oriented folds with longitudinal reverse faults on the northern flank of anticlines

## Formations (oldest to youngest)

### 1. Famenne Formation (FAM) — Famennian
- Green fissile schists with ochre fauna debris beds, alternating with siltstone beds
- **Thickness**: 250 m (Hermeton valley)
- **Recommended section**: Meander des Hallees at Gochenee

### 2. Esneux Formation (ESN) — Famennian
- Fine green to olive-green sandstones in beds of a few centimeters
- Rich in sedimentary structures: cross-stratification, load figures, bioturbation
- Contains crinoid limestone beds near the top
- **Thickness**: 250 m (Hermeton valley)

### 3. Souverain-Pre Formation (SVP) — Famennian
- Thick metric beds of nodular limestone with silto-schistose cement
- Bioclastic nodules (crinoids, brachiopods)
- **Thickness**: 50-120 m

### 4. Ciney Formation (CIN) — Upper Famennian
- Fine sandstones and siltstones with nodular limestone at base and top
- Stratified limestone at summit
- Contains three "Strunian" units marking the progressive return to carbonate sedimentation (transition to Dinantian)
- **Thickness**: 250-300 m (including 120 m Strunian at Hastiere)

### 5. Hastiere Formation (HAS) — Hastarian (Lower Carboniferous)
- Crinoidal limestone with schistose and calc-schistose beds
- Three units (bottom to top):
  - alpha: stratified crinoidal limestone with schistose recurrences, oolitic bed at base
  - beta: massive crinoidal limestone in thick beds, dark, finely crinoidal
  - gamma: alternating grey calcareous schist and crinoidal limestone in decimetric beds
- **Thickness**: 20-35 m
- **Type locality**: quarry on the Hastiere-Lavaux to Insemont road (Carriere Demanet)

### 6. Pont d'Arcole Formation (PDA) — Hastarian
- Fissile schists with brownish-ochre weathering patina
- Contains crinoidal limestone beds near the top
- Rich in bryozoans, fenestrae, crinoids, brachiopods (*Spiriferina peracuta*)
- **Thickness**: 15-20 m

### 7. Landelies Formation (LAN) — Hastarian
- Dark grey argillaceous crinoidal limestone, thick-bedded
- Rich in macrofauna debris (corals *Siphonophyllia* sp., brachiopods)
- Characteristic hydrocarbon odor when broken
- Fractures often coated with iron oxide from pyrite oxidation
- **Thickness**: 35-40 m
- Historically quarried for building stone and hydraulic lime production

### 8. Maurenne Formation (MAU) — Hastarian/Ivorian
- Calc-schists and grey argillaceous limestone
- Rich in crushed brachiopod debris, corals, and small crinoids
- Intense schistosity in places; black cherts in upper part
- **Thickness**: 15-40 m

## Structure

Paleozoic rocks describe folds with east-west oriented axes. Longitudinal reverse faults affect the northern flanks of anticlines. The Moniat anticline at Anseremme (near Dinant) is a key structural feature, with the Esneux Formation forming a tectonized core.

## Cover deposits

Surficial materials are limited to:
- Cenozoic remnants trapped in karst within Dinantian limestones
- Terrace deposits
- Alluvium from the Meuse and its tributaries
- Extensive Quaternary loess covering the plateau's Paleozoic formations

## Economic geology

- **Iron ore extraction**: historically important
- **Carboniferous limestone quarrying**: for marble production (Dinant marbles)
